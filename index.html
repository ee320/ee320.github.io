Abstract
A speech, music, silence, noise discrimination is presented in this project. The proposed algorithm needs no training phase,. and it classifies audio stream into 4 classes- speech, music, silence and noise.
1. Introduction
Over the last several years, major efforts have been made to develop methods for extracting information from audio visual media In this project we are concentrating on classification of an audio signal into vocal, music and silence and we are creating audio class histogram for the same.
1.1 Introduction to Problem
Given an audio file, we have to extract features and use them to segment and classsify the audio data into speech, music, silence and noise. And to plot a histogram for the same

1.4 Proposed Approach
In the first step the audio input is initially cut into overlapping frames of audio samples and for each frame parametric feature vectors are extracted. First silence segments are extracted from audio stream by using energy, and for differentiating between music and speech we are using 5 feature vectors which are derived from RMS and ZCR.The feature vector sequence is afterwards driven to the segmentation stage, where the feature vectors used are- Normalized RMS variance, probability of null zero-crossings,joint RMS/ZC measure,Void intervals frequency and Maximal mean frequency.
